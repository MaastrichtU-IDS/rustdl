## Open levers

These are the obvious next moves, ranked by expected payoff and
ranked by clear-go-do-it-ness (not ambition):

### 1. Selective trust-Sat verification heuristic

The `trust_sat: bool` parameter on `subsumes_via_tableau` is wired
through both call sites (top-down walk and defined-sup sweep) and
currently defaults to `true` everywhere — no behaviour change.

The simplest useful heuristic: pass `trust_sat=false` only when the
wedge runs *fast* (say, < 50 ms). A NotSubsumed verdict from the
wedge in 5 ms is more likely a "didn't try hard enough" than a
genuine model construction. Tableau-verify those.

What we already know:
- Defined-sup sweep with `trust_sat=false` on all 699 GALEN defined
  classes × ~2700 candidates was too expensive (killed after several
  hours). Need filtering to make it tractable.
- The mechanism is in place; only the policy is missing.

Estimated impact: recover most of GALEN's 109 / notgalen's 27 with
modest wall cost (probably +1–3 min on GALEN, not +hours).

### 2. Functional-role inference in the saturator

The `PathologicalCondition` pattern needs the wedge or saturator to
collapse sibling sub-properties of a functional role. EL++ has this
rule. Implementation:

- For each functional role `R`, identify all sub-properties `R_i ⊑ R`
- When a class `X` has `∃R_i.A` and `∃R_j.B` for distinct `R_i, R_j ⊑ R`,
  the witnesses are the same (functionality) — so `X ⊑ ∃R.(A ⊓ B)`
- Wire this into the saturator as a CR-rule, or as a tableau merge
  rule

Estimated impact: closes most of GALEN's `<Region>Pathology` pattern.
Probably 50–80 of the 109 MISSED. Bigger calculus investment.

### 3. ≥n cardinality with disjointness (the InterestingPizza /
   PairedBodyStructure pattern)

If `X ⊑ ∃R.B₁ ⊓ ... ⊓ ∃R.Bₙ` and the `Bᵢ` are pairwise disjoint,
then `X ⊑ ≥n R.⊤`. Currently we don't materialize this. The wedge
sometimes proves it (pizza converges in 300 ms; GALEN's analogues
might be harder).

Estimated impact: closes GALEN's PairedBodyStructure cluster (~20–30
MISSED).

### 4. Perf pivot on the saturator

The saturator is now the bottleneck on small ontologies (SIO 68 s wall
is mostly saturation; tableau probes are sub-second). Profiling
would identify hot loops. The Or-body trigger lowering added a 4×
slowdown on SIO (`fddf2ee` notes this) — could be optimised.

## What's known to not work (don't redo)

- **Defined-sup sweep with `trust_sat=false` on full GALEN**: tried,
  exceeded 8000 CPU minutes before kill. Don't do this without
  pre-filtering candidates aggressively.
- **Direct tableau probe (`RUSTDL_HYPERTABLEAU=0`) on SIO_010092 ⊑
  SIO_010410**: didn't finish in 3+ hours. Konclude has a calculus
  optimisation here that we don't have; brute force won't catch up.
- **Defaulting trust-Sat off**: kills the pizza/SIO perf wins (3 s →
  many minutes). Users who *need* completeness can set the env var.
- **Top-level disjunction on a LHS conjunctive operand**: silently
  dropped by saturator (see `lhs_conjunction_with_unsupported_operand
  _is_dropped` test). Partial lowering is unsound; full lowering
  needs full case analysis.

See `docs/hypertableau-dead-ends.md` for the historical record of
failures with diagnostics.
